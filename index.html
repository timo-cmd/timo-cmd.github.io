Working on SNOBOL and SPITBOL

Some days ago, I stumbled upon the SNOBOL4 programminmg language and got fascinated about it. It is quite awesome and amazing. SNOBOL is actually a programming language that was designed it the late 60s by Ralph E. Griswold. While languages such as Ada, Pascal or Perl showed off with new standard-syntaxes, SNOBOL had it's first shout out. SNOBOL covered a lot of unusual syntaxes. However, the usage of SNOBOL has risen dramatically. I want to explain you why: SNOBOL is an interpreted language that is based on string-proceessed data-manipulations, such as pattern-matching. Data-types in SNOBOL were presented for the first time as first-class citizens which is a big honor. As you see, SNOBOL has became a well known and great to use language.

Then in the 1970s Robert Dewar and Ken Belcher have designed the new era of SNOBOL. The two scientists who have studied at Illinois Institute of Techology, have implemented the first optimized, high-performance compiler for the SNOBOL language. They named their invention: SPITBOL (SPeedy ImplemenTation of symBOlic Language). SPITBOL used an aggressive assembler on its high-performing compiler. SPITBOL could actually run from & until to 10 times faster than the SNOBOL interpreter.

Sadly, after a short time, Perl and Ada got their first shout out on the market. They literally destroyed SNOBOL and SPITBOL because they featured new String-processing feautres which were more conventional.

However, I really love SNOBOL and the SPITBOL compiler. So my aim was it,: to recreate and redesign the SNOBOL runtime fully. I have started a project called SNOBOL5 (named after SNOBOL4 was the last released language). SNOBOL5 has a lot of useful new features that I've adapted by languages such as Crystal, visual FoxPro and SPITBOL. SNOBOL5 is actively mantained by me and is written for compiler-research. SNOBOL5 is available on its homepage: SNOBOL5.org
